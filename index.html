A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

MISSILE MAN OF INDIA

Introduction

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, widely known as A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, was an esteemed Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India." His life and work have inspired millions across the globe.

Early Life and Education

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was born into a modest Tamil Muslim family. His father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, and his mother was a housewife. Despite the financial constraints, Kalam was a bright and hardworking student. He completed his schooling at Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram and later graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. He went on to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Career in Science and Technology

After graduating from MIT, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He later transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit in 1980.

He returned to DRDO in the 1980s to lead the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), resulting in the development of missiles such as Agni and Prithvi. His contributions to science and technology were recognized with numerous awards, including the Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997).

Presidency (2002-2007)

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the opposition Indian National Congress. During his presidency, he was affectionately known as the "People's President." He endeavored to bridge the gap between the government and the people, advocating for integrity and youth empowerment.

Vision 2020

Kalam envisioned transforming India into a developed nation by 2020. His plan, known as "Vision 2020," focused on areas like education, agriculture, technology, and healthcare. He passionately believed in the power of education and innovation to drive India's development and often engaged with students and young people to inspire them to dream big.

Literary Contributions

Kalam was also a prolific writer, authoring several books that have inspired millions. Some of his notable works include:

  • Wings of Fire (1999): An autobiography detailing his early life and career.
  • Ignited Minds (2002): A call to the youth of India to realize their potential.

Legacy and Death

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His sudden demise was mourned by the entire nation and by admirers worldwide. His life and work continue to inspire generations to contribute to society with dedication, humility, and a sense of purpose.

Quotes by A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

  • "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."
  • "You have to dream before your dreams can come true."
  • "Excellence is a continuous process and not an accident."
  • "Don't take rest after your first victory because if you fail in second, more lips are waiting to say that your first victory was just luck."

Conclusion

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's journey from a small town in Tamil Nadu to becoming one of the most revered figures in India is a testament to his extraordinary dedication, vision, and humility. His legacy as a scientist, leader, and teacher continues to illuminate the path for millions, reminding us of the power of dreams, perseverance, and the relentless pursuit of knowledge.
